About Jacob

Story

Hey. I'm Jacob. If I had to sum up my personality and motivations in a short description, I would tell you this: I am a problem solver. I graduated in 2016 with a degree in Mechanical Engineering with the goal of becoming a designer. With a few years of automation systems design under my belt, I moved back into the familiar territory of R&D, this time in the world of Aerospace.

I had originally went into Mech. Eng. because I thought it would put me into a position to solve the problems at the cutting edge of what humanity is capable of. Only, as the years went by and I learned more, I realized time and time again that the cutting edge requires computation at increasingly more demanding scales. I began to realize that I needed a new set of skills in order to get myself into the position I had wanted to be in years prior. So I followed the technology and put my previous experiences and design capabilities to work to pursue software engineeering and explore solutions to big problems.

As the pandemic hit and layoffs happened, I took the opportunity to build something. I hadn't programmed anything serious in several years, but nevertheless, I pushed through the sea of things I didn't know and prototyped a real estate app in excel, written in visual basic. My love for programming was rekindled, and after deciding that I wasn't skilled enough to build the real estate app the way that it needed to be done - as a web app- I began to teach myself web development full time.

Ongoing Projects!

Hyperion Web

Hyperion Web is my brainchild. My long term goal is to turn it into a tech incubator that licenses creative software tools. In the short term, its a marketing company with a focus on getting extremely well performing sites into the hands of small business owners. Serving these clients has informed a lot of my trajectory for what value-add tooling may look like.

Collider

Collider is a big experiment in elementary component design. I have some ambitions for a full-fledged modular library that is built on the premise that complex apps and sites are built of chains of smaller sub-components, sort of like chains of molecules. My experience revisiting common sets of UI components for each site time after time has me interested in developing this for use at Hyperion, serving clients.

Quantum Thought Co.

Quantum Thought is a blog I dreamt up as a platform for me to think and write about creativity. I have a lot of experience synthesizing ideas and designs and so its really fun to dive a bit deeper into how the human brain works to achieve such creative synthesis. There is a lot of overlap with the field of computational creativity, which I am quite taken by.
